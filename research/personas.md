# User Personas — CareOS Healthcare Portal

## Research Methodology
- 12 contextual interviews (patients, caregivers, care coordinators)
- 3 rounds of usability testing
- 200+ survey responses
- Session recording analysis (FullStory)

---

## Persona 1 — "The Overwhelmed Patient"

**Name:** Maria, 47  
**Role:** Elementary school teacher  
**Tech comfort:** Medium  

### Goals
- Quickly understand what her insurance covers before a visit
- Easily find in-network specialists
- Avoid surprise medical bills

### Pain Points
- Insurance jargon is confusing ("deductible vs out-of-pocket max")
- Has to call member services for simple questions — long hold times
- Claims process feels opaque and stressful

### Behavioral Patterns
- Uses mobile 70% of the time
- Researches before calling — prefers self-service
- Highly anxious about healthcare costs

### Design Implications
- Plain-language explanations for all insurance terms
- Proactive cost estimates before appointments
- AI copilot to answer benefits questions 24/7
- Clear visual progress bars for deductible/OOP tracking

## Persona 2 — "The Active Manager"

**Name:** David, 34  
**Role:** Software engineer, manages family plan for 4 members  
**Tech comfort:** High  

### Goals
- Manage healthcare for entire family from one portal
- Track multiple prescriptions and refills
- Get in and out quickly — values efficiency

### Pain Points
- Has to log in/out of separate portals for each family member
- No consolidated view of family benefits usage
- Appointment scheduling requires too many steps

### Behavioral Patterns
- Desktop-primary user
- Wants data — charts, numbers, summaries
- Compares providers by rating and cost

### Design Implications
- Family member switcher in navigation
- Dashboard with consolidated stats
- Streamlined 3-step appointment booking
- Data visualizations for benefits usage trends

## Persona 3 — "The Senior Navigator"

**Name:** Eleanor, 68  
**Role:** Retired nurse, Medicare supplemental plan  
**Tech comfort:** Low-medium  

### Goals
- Find providers who accept her plan
- Understand her Medicare + supplemental coverage
- Get prescription cost estimates

### Pain Points
- Small text and low contrast interfaces
- Confusing multi-step flows
- Doesn't trust AI — wants human confirmation option

### Behavioral Patterns
- Tablet user, large text preference
- Reads everything carefully before acting
- Calls member services after using digital tools to confirm

### Design Implications
- WCAG 2.1 AA+ color contrast (minimum 4.5:1)
- Large tap targets (minimum 44x44px)
- Clear "Talk to a Human" escalation path alongside AI
- Simple, linear flows — no branching complexity on critical tasks
